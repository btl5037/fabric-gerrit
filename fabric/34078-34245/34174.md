Something I was just thinking about this morning is how the channel MSP is not actually a physical MSP in itself (or called as such anywhere in the code). It's a r*elationship* between the identities of channel members (which themselves are MSPs) and the enforcement of channel level policies. 

So it's an MSP in notion, rather than in substance. Which is why it has always been called a channel MSP. 

But the same logical relationship also exists for endorsement policies. These are, in a sense, transaction MSPs, since they do the same job as a channel MSP in linking an identity (again, in this sense, an Org MSP) with a role (an endorsing organization), and performs its own enforcing of the policies listed therein.
